FMZBC AV Process Basics

How volunteers prepare and serve

These summaries are adapted from the AV process documentation for volunteer training. They explain what volunteers need to understand without publishing internal account, purchasing, routing, asset, or leadership-only details.

Purpose

What AV supports

AV supports worship, livestreaming, Bible studies, ministry events, meetings, classroom AV, media production, graphics, recording, editing, and display support.

  • Know whether you are supporting a service, event, meeting, recording, or room setup.
  • Stay within your assigned role and ask the lead before changing a workflow.
  • Remember that production quality comes from preparation, training, and teamwork.
Communication

Deadlines prevent pressure

Volunteers should understand the timing standards so they can help ministries prepare before service day.

  • Sunday service information is due Friday at noon.
  • Announcement information is due Tuesday at noon.
  • General AV requests and media files should arrive at least 48 hours before an event.
  • Major event details should be shared 1-2 weeks ahead when possible.
  • Media requests usually need 3-5 business days.
Requests

What a good AV request includes

A complete request helps the team test files, plan setup, and avoid last-minute guessing.

  • Event name, ministry name, date, time, room, and contact person.
  • Microphone, media playback, livestream, recording, Zoom, setup, and stage-change needs.
  • Files submitted early enough to test before people arrive.
  • Clear final decision-maker for live changes.
Serving

Service and event flow

Most volunteer work follows the same rhythm: arrive, check, rehearse, serve, reset, and report.

  • Arrive early enough to check your assigned position.
  • Confirm content, mics, cameras, graphics, stream or recording needs with the lead.
  • Practice or rehearse before taking on live responsibility.
  • Afterward, help reset the area and report anything unusual.
Media

Graphics and media standards

Volunteer-facing media work should be readable, approved, properly timed, and safe to use online.

  • Use large readable text, strong contrast, and clean backgrounds.
  • Check names, dates, spelling, times, and approval status before playback.
  • Ask before using content when copyright or privacy is unclear.
Issues

When something goes wrong

The volunteer response is to protect the service, notify the right lead, and document the issue after the moment passes.

  • Stay calm and communicate the issue to the assigned lead.
  • Use backup or simplified options only when trained or directed.
  • Document the room, system affected, action taken, temporary fix, and follow-up need.
Private information intentionally excluded

This public training page does not include vendor account details, purchase records, login paths, private links, room power sequences, wiring or routing diagrams, network escalation details, asset records, or leadership-only approval context.

Free tools only

Recommended starting order: OpenLP or FreeShow for ProPresenter-style presentation practice, OBS Studio for streaming fundamentals, Audacity for cross-platform audio editing, GarageBand for Apple-only audio practice, and Affinity or Adobe Express free tools for basic graphics.
